Understanding the Formula Components

y1 (Starting Point)

The y-coordinate of the first known point, serving as the baseline for the calculation.

(x - x1) (Horizontal Distance)

The distance along the x-axis from the first known point to the target point.

(y2 - y1) / (x2 - x1) (Slope)

The rate of change between the two known points, representing the line's steepness.

Frequently Asked Questions

Linear interpolation is a mathematical method used to estimate unknown values that fall between two known data points. It assumes a straight-line relationship between the points and is ideal for situations where you need to find intermediate values in datasets, predict trends, or fill gaps in data series.

Linear interpolation provides exact results when the actual relationship between data points is truly linear. For non-linear relationships, it gives approximations that are most accurate when the data points are close together and the curve between them is relatively straight.
